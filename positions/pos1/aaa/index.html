Position: OPS student

The Smart Crop Phenomics Team at the Blueberry Breeding and Genomics Laboratory is looking for students interested in applying engineering solutions for classifying blueberries. The group study methods for using robotic devices for improving multimodal phenotypic data and provide plant breeders with more accurate data.

We are looking for students with experience / interest on the following areas:

  • Computer vision / A.I. (Yolo, R-CNN models, autoencoders)
  • Speech recognition / A.I. (Transformations: FFT, regression models)
  • 3D reconstruction / SLAM (Photogrammetry, Neural Radiance Field, LiDAR)
Compensation: $14 / hour (40h / week)
Field of Study: Engineering / Computer Science
Required:
  • Be able to work in loco
  • Be willing to explore and to find alternative solutions
  • Be open to participate in all the steps of the research. It may include data collection and labelling, field visit, setup of computer environment and devices, among others.
Nice to Have:
  • Programming languages: C++, Python (x86, Arm)
  • Libraries: TensorFlow, Pytorch, OpenCV, Open3D, PCL
  • Models: GAN, LSTM, VCAE, R-CNN, SSD
  • Experience using: Stereo sensor, LiDAR, VR headset
How to Apply:

This is a short-term position with possibility of extension depending on performance. Interested candidates should send CV, available schedule and areas of interest to Bruno Leme at b.caixetaleme@ufl.edu.
